The Eight Lokas

A summary of the eight realms in the Game of Self-Inquiry

01

Bhu-loka

भू-लोक cells 1-9

The physical plane. The player begins here bound by kāma (desire) and the law of karma, unaware that "I and Brahman are not-two". Mind is identified with the body and its appetites; cell 5 itself is named bhu-loka, the physical realm.

Theme

Bondage. The basic emotional drives - anger, greed, delusion, pride, dissatisfaction, lust - run the show. Every choice here pulls the player either toward purification or deeper into the cycle.

Key cells

1janma birth 2māyā 3krodha anger 4lobha greed 5bhu-loka 6moha delusion 7mada pride 8mithyā avarice 9kāma desire

Way out

Catalyzed by suffering. Kāma (cell 9) is both trap and best friend - when guided by the scriptures, desire becomes the engine of growth that propels the player into the next loka.

02

Bhuva-loka

भुव-लोक cells 10-18

The lower astral plane. Player begins tapas (discipline) and śuddhi (purification). The mind starts watching itself. Imagination, fantasy and the emotional roller-coaster of bhuva-loka (cell 14) take over the inner life.

Theme

Self-observation. Joy and envy, fantasy and stuckness, jealousy and empathy all live side by side. Player swings between expansion and contraction, but the seed of dayā (empathy, cell 17) is the great elevator out of this row.

Key cells

10tapah/śuddhi discipline 11gandharva 12īrsya envy 13antariksa stuckness 14bhuva-loka 15nāga fantasy 16mātsarya jealousy 17dayā empathy 18harsa joy

Way out

Empathy (17) is the single most powerful ladder on this row. It sweeps egotism aside and lifts the player past many traps. Joy (18) is the byproduct.

03

Svarga-loka

स्वर्ग-लोक cells 19-27

The heaven of action. The realm of karma-yoga - finding one's place in the world through doing. Action becomes deliberate, charitable, atoning, dharmic. The player learns that "results are not entirely in my hands" and that nobility means asking "What can I give?"

Theme

Right action. Heaven (svarga-loka, cell 23) is here, but it's bound by time. The whole row teaches that mistakes are lessons. Susanga (good company) lifts; kusangati (bad company) drops.

Key cells

19karma-yoga responsibility 20dāna charity 21prāyaścitta atonement 22dharma righteousness 23svarga-loka heaven 24kusangati bad company 25susanga good company 26duhkham sorrow 27paramārtha nobility

Way out

Dharma (22) plus good company (25) plus the shift from "what can I take?" to "what can I give?" (27).

04

Mahaloka

महलोक cells 28-36

The plane of balance. Svadharma (one's own path) is found. The player responds to each situation from unique strengths, not from biases. Head and heart align; mahaloka (cell 32) is itself named the balance plane.

Theme

Maturation. The senses themselves refine - taste, fragrance, even the texture of perception becomes subtler. The contrast: adharma and nāraka (hell as purification) still lurk for the player who slips. Honesty (svaccha, 36) is the foundation of everything above.

Key cells

28svadharma own path 29adharma unrighteousness 30uttamagati good tendencies 31yaksa sanctity 32mahaloka balance 33gandha 34rasa 35nāraka hell 36svaccha clear thinking

Way out

Honesty (36) about one's own strengths and weaknesses opens the door to real jñāna (self-knowledge) in the next loka.

05

Janaloka

जनलोक cells 37-45

The wisdom realm. Jñāna (self-knowledge) begins. Player no longer just does - they study, inquire, listen to a teacher, contemplate. The five prāṇas (subtle energies) are understood as the body's intelligence. Janaloka (cell 41) is named the wisdom plane.

Theme

From doing to knowing. This row is the source of most scriptural teachings. Without jñāna (37), the player falls into avidyā (ignorance, 44). With it, they attain suvidyā (direct knowledge, 45) - one of the game's liberation points.

Key cells

37jñāna self-knowledge 38prāna 39apāna 40vyāna 41janaloka wisdom 42agni 43manusya evolved being 44avidyā ignorance 45suvidyā moksha

Way out

Sustained study of self, world and Brahman (37). Reaching 45 (suvidyā) means moksha is attained - the highest human goal accomplished.

06

Tapa-loka

तप-लोक cells 46-54

The purification realm. Knowledge from Janaloka is fused with action through viveka (discernment), tapa-loka (cell 50, austerity itself), and finally bhakti (devotion). Sacred archetypes appear: Sarasvati, Yamuna, Ganga.

Theme

Living the truth one knows. It's easy to say "there is only God" - this row asks whether that vision holds up in heated arguments and difficult relationships. Bhakti (54) is the natural flowering when knowledge of Īśvara matures into love.

Key cells

46viveka discernment 47sarasvatī neutrality 48yamunā 49ganga 50tapa-loka purification 51prthivī 52himsā justified hurt 53jalam 54bhakti devotion

Way out

Devotion (54) - the recognition that devotee, devoted and act of devotion are all one Īśvara. Another liberation point in the game.

07

Satya-loka

सत्य-लोक cells 55-63

The truth plane. Aham-kāra (sense of individuality) is examined and dissolved. The player chants OM, takes a stand in truth (satya-loka, cell 59), holds subuddhi (clear intellect) through last breath.

Theme

Stabilization. Three subtle snakes remain - durbuddhi (clouded thinking, 61), the slip from sukha (62) into tamas (inertia, 63). The lesson of this row: even great clarity needs constant maintenance.

Key cells

55aham kāra 56om OM 57vāyu 58tejah 59satya-loka truth 60subuddhi clear intellect 61durbuddhi clouded thinking 62sukha 63tamah inertia

Way out

Nididhyāsana (contemplation) until the last breath. Sustain 60 (subuddhi) and the small fire of habit cannot become conflagration.

08

Brahman-loka

ब्रह्म-लोक cells 64-72

The highest realm. The One Reality is seen from every standpoint: prakṛti (substance), śiva (time/destruction), brahmā (creator), brahman (the absolute, also the finishing square at 68). The three guṇas reveal themselves as the universe's powers.

Theme

The finish. Cell 68 (brahman) is the destination - eternally existing, awareful, limitless-complete. But even here, the guṇas (sattva, rajas, tamas) can unbalance the player; tama-guna (72) is the last snake that sends them back to count from 51.

Key cells

64prakrti 65uranta dissolved ego 66ānanda bliss 67śiva 68brahman moksha 69brahmā 70sattva-guna balance 71raja-guna restlessness 72tama-guna inertia

Way out

Already here. Cell 68 is liberation - freedom and clarity while living, the end of future births, accomplished through the guru-śiṣya relationship.

The arc, at a glance

The player starts at Bhu-loka bound by desire and ends at Brahma-loka as the absolute. Each loka has a distinct flavor of practice and a distinct snake to watch for. The colors run from earth (brown) up through the rainbow to violet - matching how the board is read from bottom to top.
